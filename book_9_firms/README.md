## Section 9.1: The Fundamental Failure of Markets

From Book 8: markets oscillate, σ accumulates, clearing is temporary.

**Law 9.1 — Internal Market Instability:**

When σ from repeated exchange exceeds the lock capacity of an identity, external market interaction becomes non-viable.

Plain meaning:
- constant negotiation is expensive
- repeated pricing creates σ
- trust memory deepens cost

**Markets are optimal globally — destructive locally.**

## Section 9.3: Why Hierarchy Emerges (Not Power)

Hierarchy is not dominance. It is **σ-routing**.

**Law 9.2 — σ-Minimization by Centralization:**

Routing decisions through fewer nodes minimizes σ accumulation.

```
σ_network ∝ decision paths
```

Hierarchy:
- reduces branching
- reduces negotiation
- increases lock depth

**Hierarchy is compression, not control.**

**Role:** A persistent Φ–∇Φ–σ mapping that absorbs uncertainty so the system doesn't have to.

Roles exist because:

- specialization localizes entropy
- memory becomes reusable

**Roles are entropy sinks.**

**A firm grows until internal σ > market σ. Then it stops.**
