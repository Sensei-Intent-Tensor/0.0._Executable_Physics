## Book 7 Mandate

**How can conserved quantities be compared across identities without collapsing meaning?**

If this fails:
- accounting breaks
- exchange cannot scale
- money becomes fiction

---

## Section 7.1: Why Measurement Is Not Optional

From Book 6: conserved resource ρ, transferable capacity, repeated exchange, accounting memory.

But without measurement:
- ρ is incomparable
- exchange is local-only
- dominance cannot generalize

**Measurement is forced by scale.**

---

## Section 7.2: What Measurement Really Is

**Measurement:** The mapping of a quantity to a stable reference invariant under exchange.

```
μ: ρ → ℝ  such that  μ(ρ_a) = μ(ρ_b) ⟹ exchange equivalence
```

Measurement does not mean truth. It means **comparability**.

---

## Section 7.3: Units (Derived, Not Assumed)

**Unit:** A conserved reference packet of ρ used to normalize comparison.

Properties:
- transferable
- repeatable
- invariant under selection
- abstractable

**A unit is frozen agreement about σ-handling capacity.**

---

## Section 7.4: Standardization Pressure

If multiple units exist:
- exchange friction rises
- σ increases
- selection disfavors complexity

**Law 7.1 — Unit Convergence:**

In repeated exchange networks, units converge toward minimal σ comparison cost.

This is why currencies standardize, protocols dominate, formats win.

Not politics. Selection.

---

## Section 7.5: Abstract Tokens (Pre-Money)

Once units stabilize, identities may exchange **representations** instead of raw ρ.

**Token:** A symbolic placeholder for a unit of ρ, trusted through accounting continuity.

```
Token ↔ ρ  (via ledger)
```

Tokens allow:
- delayed exchange
- indirect exchange
- non-local exchange

**Tokens are time-shifted trust.**

---

## Section 7.6: Ledger Emergence

Tokens require memory beyond individuals.

**Ledger:** A shared memory structure that preserves token-resource correspondence.

Ledger properties:
- append-only bias (σ memory)
- dispute cost > normal exchange cost
- persistence across selections

This is the **ancestor of institutions**.

---

## Section 7.7: Money (Finally, Precisely)

**Money:** A standardized, abstract, conserved token representing transferable σ-handling capacity, enforced by a persistent ledger.

Money is not:
❌ value  
❌ wealth  
❌ power

Money **measures and moves** resource capacity.

---

## Section 7.8: Price (Derived)

**Price:** The ratio of σ reduction achievable per unit of money in a given exchange context.

```
Price = Δσ / Δρ
```

Prices vary because:
- σ landscapes differ
- locks differ
- histories differ

Not because of belief.

---

## Section 7.9: Inflation & Collapse (Preview)

If:
- tokens exceed backing ρ
- ledger trust degrades
- σ spikes

Then:
- prices decouple
- selection shifts
- systems reset

Inflation is **ledger drift**, not greed.
